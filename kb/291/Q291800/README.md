## Q291800: Fatal Exception Error when Attempting Multiplayer Connection

{% raw %}

	Article: Q291800
	Product(s): Microsoft Home Games
	Version(s): 
	Operating System(s): 
	Keyword(s): kbimu
	Last Modified: 18-JAN-2002
	
	-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
	The information in this article applies to:
	
	- Microsoft MechWarrior 4: Vengeance 
	- Microsoft Links LS 2000 
	-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
	
	SYMPTOMS
	========
	
	When attempting to play a multiplayer game by using TCP/IP or Gaming Zone with
	one of the programs listed at the beginning of this article, you may see a blue
	screen containing a message similar to the following:
	
	  OE: Page fault occurred at 028:DCDC2FF5 in VXD Win95ac.
	
	The memory address may vary.
	
	CAUSE
	=====
	
	This behavior may occur if either of the following is true:
	
	- The system modem is using outdated drivers.
	
	  -or-
	
	- The system modem is damaged.
	
	RESOLUTION
	==========
	
	To resolve this problem, download and install updated drivers for your modem.
	
	For information about how to contact your modem manufacturer, click the
	appropriate article number below to view the article in the Microsoft Knowledge
	Base:
	
	  Q65416 Hardware and Software Third-Party Vendor Contact List, A-K
	
	  Q60781 Hardware and Software Third-Party Vendor Contact List, L-P
	
	  Q60782 Hardware and Software Third-Party Vendor Contact List, Q-Z
	
	If the updated drivers do not resolve this problem, you may need to replace the
	modem.
	
	MORE INFORMATION
	================
	
	To determine the manufacturer of your modem, follow these steps:
	
	1. Click Start, point to Settings, and then click Control Panel.
	
	2. Double-click the System icon.
	
	3. Click the Device Manager tab.
	
	4. Double-click Modem. In the drop-down list, you will see the model of your
	  modem.
	
	5. Click OK.
