## Q253937: FS2000: Program Hangs When You Fly Over San Francisco

	Article: Q253937
	Product(s): Microsoft Home Games
	Version(s): WINDOWS:
	Operating System(s): 
	Keyword(s): kb3rdparty kbdisplay kbimu msgame
	Last Modified: 07-MAY-2002
	
	-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
	The information in this article applies to:
	
	- Microsoft Flight Simulator 2000 Professional Edition 
	-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
	
	SYMPTOMS
	========
	
	When you fly over downtown San Francisco, Microsoft Flight Simulator 2000
	Professional Edition may stop responding (hang).
	
	CAUSE
	=====
	
	This behavior can occur if the following conditions are true:
	
	- An ATI 3D Rage Pro video adapter is installed in the computer.
	
	- The video driver for the ATI 3D Rage Pro video adapter is outdated.
	
	RESOLUTION
	==========
	
	To resolve this issue, download and install the latest ATI 3D Rage Pro video
	driver for your video adapter from the following ATI Web site:
	
	  http://support.atitech.ca
	
	MORE INFORMATION
	================
	
	You may need to remove the outdated ATI 3D Rage Pro video driver from the
	computer before you install the latest video driver. To do this:
	
	1. Click Start, point to Settings, and then click Control Panel.
	
	2. Double-click Add/Remove Programs.
	
	3. Click ATI Display Driver, and then click Add/Remove.
	
	4. When you are prompted to restart Windows, do so.
