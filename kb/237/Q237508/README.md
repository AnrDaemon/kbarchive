## Q237508: Encarta 2000: Problems When You Turn On Closed Captions

{% raw %}

	Article: Q237508
	Product(s): Microsoft Home Multimedia Titles
	Version(s): 
	Operating System(s): 
	Keyword(s): kbenable kbui kbimu kbEnableHear
	Last Modified: 25-JUN-2000
	
	-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
	The information in this article applies to:
	
	- Microsoft Encarta Encyclopedia 2000 
	-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
	
	SYMPTOMS
	========
	
	When you turn on Closed Captions in Microsoft Encarta Encyclopedia 2000 and then
	attempt to play a sound or video clip, or start an InterActivity, you may
	experience one or more of the following symptoms:
	
	- The play button below the sound or video clip window is unavailable.
	
	- You cannot drag the sound or video progress bar.
	
	- If the sound is a spoken word recording, only the first sentence or sentence
	  fragment of what is spoken is visible.
	
	- If you attempt to start an InterActivity, the InterActivity does not start.
	
	CAUSE
	=====
	
	This behavior can occur if a sound card is not installed in your computer.
	
	STATUS
	======
	
	Microsoft has confirmed this to be a problem in Microsoft Encarta Encyclopedia
	2000.
	
	MORE INFORMATION
	================
	
	To determine if a sound card is installed in your computer:
	
	1. Click Start, point to Settings, and then click Control Panel.
	
	2. Double-click System, and then click the Device Manager tab.
	
	3. Click the PLUS SIGN (+) next to "Sound, video, and game controllers" to
	  expand the branch.
	
	  The manufacturer and model of the sound card installed in your computer are
	  displayed in the expanded branch.
	
	  NOTE: If a "Sound, video, and game controllers" branch is not listed in Device
	  Manager, a sound card is not installed in your computer.
