## Q175345: Age Of Empires Err Msg: Not Enough Memory or Swap File Space

	Article: Q175345
	Product(s): Microsoft Home Games
	Version(s): WINDOWS:1.0
	Operating System(s): 
	Keyword(s): kbenv kberrmsg kbsetup kbusage aoe kbimu msgamekbfaq
	Last Modified: 08-NOV-2001
	
	-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
	The information in this article applies to:
	
	- Microsoft Age of Empires, version 1.0 
	-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
	
	SYMPTOMS
	========
	
	When you attempt to start Age of Empires, you may receive the following error
	message:
	
	  Not enough memory or swap file space. Close other applications and/or free
	  some disk space, and then restart Age of Empires
	
	CAUSE
	=====
	
	This behavior can occur if either of the following conditions are true:
	
	- You have less than 16 megabytes (MB) of random access memory (RAM) available
	  in your computer.
	
	- You have less than 50 MB of available hard disk space in your computer.
	
	RESOLUTION
	==========
	
	To resolve this issue, increase the amount of available memory or hard disk
	space in your computer.
	
	To increase the amount of available memory in your computer, restart the
	computer, quit any programs that are running before you start Age of Empires, or
	install additional memory in your computer.
	
	To determine the amount of memory installed in your computer, follow these
	steps:
	
	1. Click Start, point to Settings, and then click Control Panel.
	
	2. Double-click System.
	
	3. On the General tab, note the amount of memory under Computer, and then click
	  OK.
	
	To increase the amount of available hard disk space in your computer, follow
	these steps:
	
	1. On the desktop, right-click Recycle Bin, click Empty Recycle Bin, and then
	  click Yes.
	
	2. Back up and then remove any files on the hard disk that you do not use
	  frequently. For information about how to back up your files, click Start, and
	  then click Help. On the Index tab, type "backing" (without the quotation
	  marks), and then double-click the "Backing up files" or "Backing up files,
	  folders" topic.
	
	3. Delete any unnecessary files on the hard disk. For information about how to
	  delete files, click Start, and then click Help. On the Index tab, type
	  "deleting" (without the quotation marks), and then double-click the "Deleting
	  files or folders" or "Deleting files, folders" topic.
