## Q146877: Mac Encarta: Characters Display Wrong When Copied and Pasted

	Article: Q146877
	Product(s): Microsoft Home Multimedia Titles
	Version(s): 1994 edition
	Operating System(s): 
	Keyword(s): 
	Last Modified: 26-MAY-2001
	
	-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
	The information in this article applies to:
	
	- Microsoft Encarta 1994 The Complete Multimedia Encyclopedia 
	-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
	
	SYMPTOMS
	========
	
	When you copy text from Encarta and paste the text into another program, some
	characters may not display correctly. Strange characters may be displayed in
	place of the original characters you copied.
	
	CAUSE
	=====
	
	The font selected in the application or document you are pasting to is something
	other than V1 LucidaSans.
	
	RESOLUTION
	==========
	
	The correct copied text from Encarta, do the following:
	
	1. In the program or document you are pasting to, select the pasted text.
	
	2. Change the font in your program or document to V1 LucidaSans.
	
	MORE INFORMATION
	================
	
	The text you copied from Encarta contains special or extended characters.
	Examples of special or extended characters are:
	
	- Quotation mark
	
	- Grave
	
	- Acute
	
	- Circumflex
	
	- Umlaut
	
	- Tilde
