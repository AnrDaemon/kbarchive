## Q242519: Age of Empires II: Stops Responding When You Press ALT+ TAB

	Article: Q242519
	Product(s): Microsoft Home Games
	Version(s): WINDOWS:2.0; :
	Operating System(s): 
	Keyword(s): kb3rdparty kbdisplay kbimu msgame
	Last Modified: 12-JUN-2001
	
	-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
	The information in this article applies to:
	
	- Microsoft Age of Empires II: The Age of Kings, version 2.0 
	- Microsoft Age of Empires II Expansion: The Conquerors 
	-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
	
	SYMPTOMS
	========
	
	When you press ALT+TAB to switch from another program to Microsoft Age of
	Empires II: The Age of Kings, or when you change the screen resolution in the
	game, you may experience any of the following symptoms:
	
	- The computer may stop responding (hang).
	
	- The pointer may disappear.
	
	- The screen may become distorted.
	
	CAUSE
	=====
	
	This behavior can occur if one of the following video adapters is installed in
	your computer:
	
	- Creative Labs Graphics Blaster Exxtreme
	- Hercules Stingray 128/3D
	- Matrox MGA Millennium
	- Matrox Millennium II PowerDesk
	
	RESOLUTION
	==========
	
	To resolve this issue, use the appropriate method for your video adapter.
	
	Creative Labs Graphics Blaster Exxtreme
	---------------------------------------
	
	Download and install the latest version of the video driver for the Creative Labs
	Graphics Blaster Exxtreme video adapter from the following Creative Labs Web
	site:
	
	  http://www.soundblaster.com
	
	Hercules Stingray 128/3D
	------------------------
	
	Download and install the latest version of the video driver for the Hercules
	Stingray 128/3D video adapter from the following Hercules Web site:
	
	  http://www.hercules.com
	
	Matrox MGA Millennium or Matrox Millennium II PowerDesk
	-------------------------------------------------------
	
	Download and install the latest version of the video driver for the Matrox
	Millennium II PowerDesk video adapter or the Matrox MGA Millennium video adapter
	from the following Matrox Web site:
	
	  http://www.matrox.com
