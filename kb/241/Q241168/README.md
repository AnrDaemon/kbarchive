## Q241168: NBA Inside Drive 2000: Court and Players Not Displayed Properly

	Article: Q241168
	Product(s): Microsoft Home Games
	Version(s): WINDOWS:1.0
	Operating System(s): 
	Keyword(s): kb3rdparty kbdisplay kbenv kbgraphic kbimu msgame
	Last Modified: 10-SEP-1999
	
	-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
	The information in this article applies to:
	
	- Microsoft NBA Inside Drive 2000, version 1.0 
	-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
	
	SYMPTOMS
	========
	
	When you play Microsoft NBA Inside Drive 2000, you may experience any of the
	following symptoms:
	
	- The court floor may not be displayed properly.
	
	- Multiple court floors may appear superimposed over one another.
	
	- Player textures may randomly disappear.
	
	CAUSE
	=====
	
	This behavior can occur if a Diamond Stealth 3D 2000 video adapter is installed
	in your computer.
	
	RESOLUTION
	==========
	
	You may be able to resolve this issue by installing the most recent video driver
	for your video adapter. For information about how to obtain and install the most
	recent video driver for your video adapter, contact your hardware manufacturer.
	
	For information about how to contact your hardware manufacturer, click the
	appropriate article number below to view the article in the Microsoft Knowledge
	Base:
	
	  Q65416 Hardware and Software Third-Party Vendor Contact List, A-K
	
	  Q60781 Hardware and Software Third-Party Vendor Contact List, L-P
	
	  Q60782 Hardware and Software Third-Party Vendor Contact List, Q-Z
	
	If the issue continues to occur after you install the most recent video driver
	for your video adapter, configure NBA Inside Drive 2000 to run in software
	mode:
	
	1. On the NBA Inside Drive 2000 startup screen, click Options.
	
	2. Click the Video tab.
	
	3. Under 3D Graphics Driver, click Software.
	
	4. Click OK.
