## Q303235: Offline E-mail Feature Does Not Appear When Existing MSNIA User

	Article: Q303235
	Product(s): The Microsoft Network
	Version(s): 6.1
	Operating System(s): 
	Keyword(s): 
	Last Modified: 02-JUL-2001
	
	-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
	The information in this article applies to:
	
	- The Microsoft Network version 6.1 
	-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
	
	SYMPTOMS
	========
	
	You may not see the Read mail offline box in MSN Explorer 6.1 if you upgraded to
	MSN Explorer 6.1 with existing MSN Internet Access account over a Local Area
	Network (LAN) connection.
	
	CAUSE
	=====
	
	This is a known issue.
	
	RESOLUTION
	==========
	
	To change from LAN connection to dial-up and back to LAN
	
	1. Start MSN Explorer.
	
	2. On the sign-in screen, click Connection Settings.
	
	3. Click Change how you connect to the Internet, and then click Continue.
	
	4. Select MSN dial-up Internet Access, and then click Continue.
	
	5. Check that the dialing options are configured correctly, and then click
	  Continue.
	  A message appears stating that no local numbers were found for your area.
	
	6. Click OK.
	
	7. Select Search for a new access number.
	
	8. Enter your telephone number, and then click Continue.
	  MSN Explorer will now dial a toll free number. Once connected, a list of
	  access numbers in your area will appear.
	
	9. Select the Use box for any local numbers, and clear the Use box for numbers
	  that are long distance.
	
	10. If necessary, select the Dial 1 or Area Code boxes.
	
	11. Click Continue.
	
	12. Complete Setup.
	  MSN Explorer will dial and sign you in.
	
	13. After you are signed in, click Sign Out.
	
	14. From the sign-in screen, click Connection Settings.
	
	15. Click Change how you connect to the Internet, and then click Continue
	
	16. Click Existing Internet Service Provider, and then click Continue.
	  You will now be configured to use your LAN connection, and the offline e-mail
	  feature should now appear when you sign in.
