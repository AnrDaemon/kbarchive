TITLE   :Bookshelf 98: Copyright Information Not Printed with Articles
PRODUCT :Microsoft Home Multimedia Titles
PROD/VER:WINDOWS:
OPER/SYS:
KEYWORDS:kbmm kbprint

======================================================================
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The information in this article applies to:

 - Microsoft Bookshelf 98 for Windows 
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------

SYMPTOMS
========

When you print an article from Bookshelf 98, the copyright information that is
usually printed after the end of the article may not be printed or may be
printed incompletely.

CAUSE
=====

This problem occurs when the final page of the printout contains only a single
line of article text.

RESOLUTION
==========

To work around this problem, change the size of the article text before you
print the article. To do so, use the following steps:

1. On the Tools menu, click Options.

2. Under Text Size, click a different size than the text size currently
   selected, and then click OK.

STATUS
======

Microsoft has confirmed this to be a problem in Microsoft Bookshelf 98. We are
researching this problem and will post new information here in the Microsoft
Knowledge Base as it becomes available.

MORE INFORMATION
================

This problem may occur when you print the Encyclopedia articles titled "Leonardo
da Vinci" and "Skiing" to the following printers:

 - Epson Stylus Color ESC/P 2

 - HP DeskJet 500

 - HP DeskJet 510

 - HP DeskJet 540

 - HP DeskJet 550C

 - HP DeskJet 560C
